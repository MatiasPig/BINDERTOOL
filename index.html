Binder Tool was born after gifting my daughter an Eevee plush. I wanted to build a binder with the memories of that moment using the Michi MethodThe Michi Method is a way to turn several binder pockets into one larger image. Instead of placing a different photo in each pocket, a single photo is divided into perfectly aligned sections that come together across the page.

You choose the pockets, the image, and the layout. The tool handles the exact measurements, spacing, and print margins needed so every piece fits correctly and nothing important gets cut off. — merging pockets to print bigger photos — but the tool didn't exist. So I built it.

More than a binder.

If you've ever collected cards, you know the feeling: sliding one into its pocket, turning the page, and choosing exactly where it belongs. A binder is more than storage — it's a personal archive.

Now, a single photo can span two or more pockets, turning a page into something bigger and more meaningful. You choose the layout; we handle the precise measurements and print margins so nothing important gets cut off.
